# Andragogical Audit Report: AI Tree Landing Page
**Date:** January 28, 2026
**Auditor:** Adult Learning Specialist
**Framework:** Malcolm Knowles' Andragogy Principles

---

## Executive Summary

**Overall Andragogical Alignment Score: 6.5/10**

The AI Tree landing page demonstrates **moderate alignment** with adult learning principles. While it excels in providing clear structure and interactive demonstrations, it suffers from **insufficient articulation of practical value**, **unclear time investment**, and **weak connection to professional problem-solving contexts**. The page would benefit adult learners more by leading with the "why" before the "what."

**Primary Concern:** The page treats AI learning as an academic exercise rather than a professional capability building tool. Busy professionals may struggle to justify the time investment without clearer ROI statements.

## 6. Content Structure for Adult Learners

### Current Structure: ‚ö†Ô∏è Needs Improvement

**Current Flow:**
1. Hero (tree metaphor)
2. View options
3. Beginner path CTA
4. Demos (tokenizer, vectors)
5. Content (roots ‚Üí leaves)

**Problem:** Doesn't follow adult learning design principles:
- No problem statement
- No learning objectives
- Value proposition is buried
- Time investment unclear

### üîß Recommended Structure:

```
1. HOOK (Problem + Urgency)
   "AI is changing every industry. Don't get left behind."

2. PROMISE (Outcome)
   "In 2 hours, you'll understand AI deeply enough to:
    - Train your team confidently
    - Evaluate AI vendors critically
    - Make informed strategic decisions"

3. PROOF (Social proof + Credibility)
   "Used by 2,000+ tech trainers and L&D professionals"

4. PATH (How it works)
   "4 levels, 31 concepts, learn linearly or jump around"

5. PERSONALIZATION (Choose your path)
   "Are you: A Trainer | A Manager | A Developer?"

6. CONTENT (The actual material)
   Roots ‚Üí Trunk ‚Üí Branches ‚Üí Leaves

7. APPLICATION (What to do next)
   "Now go build/teach/evaluate something real"
```

This follows the **PCPA framework** (Problem, Consequence, Promise, Action) used in professional training design.

## 17. Conclusion

The AI Tree landing page is a **high-quality educational resource** with **excellent visual design** and **solid conceptual explanations**. However, it currently serves adults **despite** its design, not **because** of it.

### The Core Issue:
The page treats AI learning as an **academic exercise** rather than **professional capability building**. Busy adults need to know:
1. **What problem** this solves (not stated clearly)
2. **How much time** it will take (unclear)
3. **What they'll be able to do** after (vague)
4. **Why this approach** vs. alternatives (not justified)

### The Opportunity:
By adding stronger **problem-framing**, **time transparency**, **professional context**, and **validation mechanisms**, AI Tree can become the **go-to resource** for tech trainers and professionals needing AI literacy.

### Recommended Focus:
**Fix the "Readiness" and "Need to Know" gaps first.** These are critical blockers preventing professionals from committing time to learn. Everything else is secondary.
